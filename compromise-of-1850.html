Compromise of 1850

The Compromise of 1850 was a combination of five bills passed by the United States Congress in September 1850 that assisted in calming down the Sectional Tension between the North and the South in regards to the new land acquired from Mexico in the Mexican-American War. It instituted Popular Soveriengty in the new territories acquired. It also instituted the Fugitive Slave Act in september 18, 1850, requiring all citizens to aid in the returning of fugitive slaves. It also made a new court system in which fugitive slaves could be tried. The judge got 5 dollars if the person was innocent, and 10 dollars if they were a slave.
