Declaration of Independence

The declaration of Independence is a document created in June of 1776 that stated that America wanted to become independent from Great Britain. The creators of the declaration known as the founding fathers consist of Thomas Jefferson of Virginia, Roger Sherman of Connecticut, Benjamin Franklin of Pennsylvania, Robert R. Livingston of New York, and John Adams of Massachusetts. Generally the declaration talked about all of the wrong doings king George III had done to the people of the American colonies. The declaration of independence sparked the revolutionary war in 1783.
